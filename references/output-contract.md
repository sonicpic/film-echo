# Film Echo Output Contract

## Review Card Template

Use this exact substrate before drafting:

- Aftertaste: the lingering emotion or mood that stays after the film.
- Core judgment: the main claim or central tension the user wants the piece to make.
- Supporting scene or detail: 2-4 anchors that can carry the claim, with at least some evidence diversity when available.
- Resistance or uncertainty: what complicates the claim, remains opaque, or pushes back against it.
- Personal resonance: why this film matters to the user, or what it touches outside the film.
- Closing landing point: the note the essay should return to at the end.

Do not draft until the cards feel user-owned rather than agent-invented.

## Truthfulness Rules

Separate these clearly in your own reasoning:
- what came from the autonomous research pack
- what the user directly remembered
- what was minimally grounded about the film
- what the user inferred
- what you are tentatively inferring from the conversation
- what still remains unknown

If the film remains partially opaque, preserve that opacity honestly.
Do not overstate symbolism, coherence, or certainty just to make the essay sound mature.
Do not flatter the user's take by pretending every reaction is equally well supported.
Do not turn an interview prompt or assistant guess into a fake film fact.
Do not let public-discourse material silently replace the user's own perspective.

## Length Bands

- Short: 600-900 Chinese-character-equivalent prose length. Use when the user has one strong feeling and a limited but vivid set of anchors.
- Standard: 1000-1600. Use for the default full reflection with one stable main thread and enough support.
- Deep: 1800-2600. Use when the user brings several scenes, formal observations, or a layered contradiction worth sustaining.

Pick the shortest band that can do justice to the material.

## Draft Shape

Default movement:
1. Start from aftertaste, a charged image, or a small detail that opens the experience.
2. Name the central judgment or tension early.
3. Develop the judgment through concrete evidence.
4. Let resistance or uncertainty remain visible if it is part of the truth.
5. Fold the film back into a personal or reflective landing.

The final piece must be:
- continuous prose
- first-person Chinese by default
- coherent from paragraph to paragraph
- light on plot summary
- grounded in concrete remembered material
- shaped by a deep interview rather than one isolated answer
- supported by more than theme-only evidence when the interview gathered formal material
- readable as both a personal record and a public-facing review

The final piece must not:
- read like a report or lecture note
- rely on bullet points in the body
- overuse second-person or third-person framing
- pad itself with generic praise, awards, or film-history filler
- pretend uncertainty has been solved when it has not

## Automatic Revision Checklist

Before drafting, make sure the interview has already passed this gate:
- at least four opened threads
- at least two distinct scene anchors
- at least two covered formal buckets unless the user explicitly requested a quick thematic pass
- at least one pressure-tested judgment
- at least one explicit revisit
- at least one unresolved knot preserved

Run one silent pass after the first draft and fix anything that fails:

- Does the opening begin from feeling, judgment, or a meaningful image instead of generic setup?
- Can the main thread be stated in one sentence?
- Does each major paragraph map back to confirmed cards or concrete anchors?
- Are the strongest claims supported by remembered details?
- Are the strongest claims supported by a mix of evidence types rather than mood alone when the material allows it?
- Are contradictions or doubts preserved when they are the point?
- Are transitions smooth enough to read as an essay rather than stitched answers?
- Is the language recognizably close to the user's stance?
- Are external facts brief and clearly serving the user's claim?
- Does any paragraph sound as if it is flattering the user instead of examining the film?
- Is there at least one place where the piece admits pressure, resistance, or ambiguity if the material requires it?

If the draft feels too tidy, check whether it has erased the user's real tension.

## Optional Humanizer Pass

If a companion skill directory named `humanizer-zh` is available, invoke `$humanizer-zh` after the Film Echo revision pass and before returning the final text.

That pass should remove AI-writing traces such as:
- filler transitions
- symmetrical or overly clean sentence patterns
- generic uplift or summary language
- slogan-like closing lines
- obvious "assistant" phrasing

After the humanizer pass, verify that the draft still preserves:
- first-person ownership
- concrete remembered details
- analytical honesty
- unresolved knots where they belong
- the user's emotional texture rather than a generic polished voice
